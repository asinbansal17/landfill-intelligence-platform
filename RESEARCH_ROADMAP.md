## SECTION 1: SWEDEN RESEARCH ROADMAP

### Week 3: Sweden Deep-Dive (3.5 hours)

#### Research Question 1: Current State
**Find:** Sweden's current waste management statistics

**Questions to Answer:**
- What is Sweden's current recycling rate? (Expected: 99%)
- How many landfills does Sweden have? (Expected: 1-2)
- What is Sweden's daily waste per capita? (Expected: ~1 kg/person/day)
- Where does waste go? (99% recycled/composted, 1% landfill)
- How many waste-to-energy plants? (Expected: 33)
- How much waste is incinerated for energy? (Expected: 50%)

**Search Terms:**
- "Sweden recycling rate statistics"
- "Sweden waste management 2024"
- "Swedish EPA waste data"
- "Sweden landfills number"

**Sources:**
- Swedish Environmental Protection Agency (Naturvårdsverket)
- World Bank waste management data
- Eurostat waste statistics
- News articles: "Sweden waste management success"

**Where to Record:** Create document called "SWEDEN_RESEARCH.md"

---

#### Research Question 2: How They Achieved It
**Find:** Sweden's timeline and policies that led to 99% recycling

**Questions to Answer:**
- When did Sweden start their waste system? (1975)
- What was the starting recycling rate? (5-10%)
- What phases did they go through? (Phase 1: Infrastructure, Phase 2: EPR, Phase 3: Zero waste)
- How long for each phase? (Phase 1: 20 years, Phase 2: 15 years, Phase 3: 10 years)
- What was the first major policy? (Ban on organic waste in landfills, 1975)
- What was the second major policy? (Extended Producer Responsibility, 1995)
- What was the third major policy? (Waste tax + incineration-to-energy, 2010)

**Search Terms:**
- "Sweden waste management history"
- "Extended Producer Responsibility Sweden"
- "Sweden waste tax policy"
- "Swedish waste management timeline"

**Sources:**
- Swedish EPA historical reports
- Academic papers on Swedish waste policy
- News articles about Swedish waste evolution
- EU case studies on Sweden

**Where to Record:** "SWEDEN_RESEARCH.md" - Section: Timeline

#### Research Question 3: Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)
**Find:** Detailed explanation of Sweden's EPR system

**Questions to Answer:**
- How does EPR work exactly? (Producers pay recycling fee for packaging)
- Who pays? (Companies that create packaging)
- How much do they pay? (Fee per unit: e.g., $0.05 per bottle)
- Who collects the fees? (Government agency)
- How is the money used? (Fund recycling infrastructure, centers, collection)
- What happened as a result? (Companies reduced packaging, recycling increased)
- How much did companies reduce packaging? (30%)
- What was the impact on prices? (Increased 5-10%)

**Search Terms:**
- "Sweden Extended Producer Responsibility how it works"
- "EPR system Sweden packaging"
- "Swedish packaging fee"

**Sources:**
- PRO Svenska (Swedish EPR organization)
- EU EPR case studies
- Academic papers on EPR

**Where to Record:** "SWEDEN_RESEARCH.md" - Section: EPR System

#### Root Cause 2: Weak Enforcement

**Questions to Answer:**
- Does the law exist? (Yes - Solid Waste Management Rules 2016)
- Is the law good? (Yes, considered comprehensive)
- Why isn't it enforced?
  - How many inspectors? (Expected: ~100 for 3,100 landfills)
  - Are they equipped? (Budget, training, authority?)
  - Corruption? (Are officials bribed to ignore violations?)
- What are penalties? ($100 or less, but violations cost millions)
- Are penalties enforced? (Rarely)

**Search Terms:**
- "Solid Waste Management Rules 2016 India enforcement"
- "India waste law implementation gaps"
- "Landfill inspectors India corruption"

**Sources:**
- Government audit reports
- NGO investigations
- News articles on enforcement
- Academic papers on implementation gaps

## RESEARCH TIPS

✅ **DO:**
- Note your sources (URL, date accessed)
- Look for official government data first
- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Record exact numbers (don't estimate)
- Save PDFs of important reports

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use only Wikipedia (use as starting point only)
- Cite news without checking facts
- Estimate numbers
- Mix up different years' data
- Assume all sources are accurate
